Receive your notice of assessment

After you’ve filed the returns for the person who died, you will receive your notice of assessments. Learn more about how to understand your notice of assessment.

Pay any balance owing

If the notice of assessment shows that the person who died has a balance owing from previous returns, make a payment to the CRA. When you make a payment, indicate the SIN of the person who died as the account you want to make the payment to. To confirm a payment has been made or to resolve an issue with a payment, see Confirm a payment.

If the estate is not able to pay the balance owing, find out how to manage an esate in debt.

Request a reassessment

T1 Income Tax and Benefit return

You can request a reassessment of the final return in situations where they have filed using the previous year’s tax package.

For example, the final return may be filed in 2022, the year of the person's death. The return generally will be processed in that year, using tax legislation applicable to the 2021 tax year. You can request a reassessment of the return in the following year (2023) to apply any tax changes introduced for the 2022 tax year.

To request a T1 reassessment, use form T1-ADJ T1 Adjustment Request.

T3 Trust Income Tax and Information return

If you need to change a T3 return after you send it to us, do not file another return for that tax year.

To request a T3 reassessment, use form T3-ADJ T3 Adjustment Request. Indicate the trust's account number, the tax year you want us to change, and attach any supporting documents.

Determine if you need a clearance certificate

After you've received the notice of assessments and paid any debts to the CRA, you can decide if you need to apply for a clearance certificate to distribute assets of the estate.
