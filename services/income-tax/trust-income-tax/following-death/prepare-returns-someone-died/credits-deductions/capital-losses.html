Net capital losses

Capital losses can be used to reduce your overall taxable income in the final return.

Gather all eligible capital losses for the final return

In addition to capital losses from January 1 to the date of death, other capital losses can also be included in the final return for someone who died if they were unclaimed in previous tax years or from the first year of a Graduated Rate Estate.

Net capital losses from the final year

Refer to Belongings, property, and investments for capital gains to calculate capital gains for the final return.

If you calculate a loss on line 19900 of Schedule 3, you can apply the capital loss against other types of income in the final return.

Unclaimed capital losses from previous tax years

If the deceased has unclaimed capital losses from previous tax years, they can usually be found in Represent a Client under "Carryover amounts" in the Tax returns section.

How to adjust unclaimed capital losses from previous tax years for the final return

Carry back capital losses from the first year of a GRE - 164(6)

If you have not yet filed the final return, skip this section. The legal representative will need to amend the final return after filing the T3 return for the estate.

If the estate disposes of capital property, the result may be a net capital loss on a T3 Trust Income Tax and Information Return. Additionally, if the estate disposes of depreciable property, the result may be a terminal loss on the T3.

In the first tax year of a deceased person's graduated rate estate, the legal representative can elect to treat all or part of these losses as losses of the deceased on the deceased's final return.

A net capital loss realized in this first tax year cannot be applied to any tax year before the year of death. For more information, refer to "164(6) election" in Chapter 3 of the T4013, T3 Trust Guide.

When filing the amended T1 final return of the deceased, you must clearly identify at the top of the return that you are amending it as a 164(6) election or a 164(6.1) election.

  • If you are applying a loss carried back from the T3, you can subtract it directly from the line 12700 amount on the amended return. There is no need to adjust it or report it on a separate line.
  • Apply losses from the 164(6) election to other forms of income, as if you were applying other losses from the final year.

Apply net capital losses against capital gains in the final return

If there is a capital gain on line 12700 of the return, you must first apply available capital losses against this capital gain.

If you have any unclaimed capital losses from previous tax years, it must be applied before any current year losses.

Adjust unclaimed losses from previous tax years

To apply an unclaimed capital loss from a previous tax year, you must first:

  1. Adjust the unclaimed prior year capital loss by the adjustment factor for the year in which it was incurred.
    • This is required to ensure the calculated capital loss from other periods is equal to what it would be in the year of the final return
  2. Apply adjusted capital losses on line 25300 up to the full amount of capital gains reported on line 12700.
  3. If there are remaining unclaimed losses, readjust the remainder back to before you applied the adjustment factor with the readjustment factor.
Inclusion Rates and Adjustment Factors for capital losses
Tax year Inclusion Rate Adjustment Factor Readjustment Factor
Before May 22, 1988 1/2 1 (no adjustment) 1 (no adjustment)
In 1988 and 1989 2/3 3/4 4/3
From 1990 to 1999 3/4 2/3 3/2
In 2000 Variable - check in Represent a Client 1 / (2 x [Inclusion Rate for 2000]) 2 x [Inclusion Rate for 2000]
From 2001 to 2021 1/2 1 (no adjustment) 1 (no adjustment)
Example of adjustments to unclaimed losses from previous years

A woman died in August of 2021. You have these details about her tax matters:

  • Capital loss in 1999: $24,000
  • Taxable capital gain in 2021: $ 6,000
  • Capital gains deductions claimed to date: $ 4,000

In the original return for 1999, the net capital losses were calculated as:

  • $24,000 (capital loss in 1999)
  • multiplied by 3/4 (inclusion rate for 1999)
  • equals $18,000 (net capital loss from 1999, never applied)

This leaves an unclaimed net capital loss of $18,000 to be carried forward to other tax years.

You decide to use the 1999 loss to reduce the 2021 taxable capital gain and to use any amount left to reduce other income for 2021.

You have to adjust the 1999 net capital loss before you can apply it. Multiply it by 2/3 to get the adjusted net capital loss:

  • $18,000 (net capital loss in 1999, never applied)
  • multiplied by 2/3 (adjustment factor for 1999)
  • equals $12,000 (adjusted net capital losses to be applied against capital gains)

Note that $12,000 is 1/2 of the original capital loss from 1999 of $24,000. The Inclusion Rate for 2001 and later years is 1/2.

To reduce the 2021 taxable capital gain, use the lesser of:

  • $12,000 (adjusted net capital loss)
  • $6,000 (2021 taxable capital gain)

After you use $6,000 of the loss to reduce capital gains on line 12700 to zero, you still have $6,000 ($12,000 – $6,000) remaining in unclaimed capital losses. You can use this amount to reduce the deceased’s other income for 2021.

To determine the amount to use, you have to readjust the $6,000. Because the loss occurred in 1999, multiply the amount left by 3/2 to get the readjusted balance:

  • $6,000 (remainder of adjusted capital losses to be applied against capital gains)
  • multiplied by 3/2 (readjustment factor for 1999)
  • equals $9,000 (readjusted capital losses)

From the readjusted balance, subtract all capital gains deductions claimed to date:

  • $9,000 (readjusted capital losses)
  • minus $4,000 (all capital gains deductions claimed in previous tax years)
  • equals $5,000 (net capital losses to be applied to other income in the final return)

You can use $5,000 to reduce the deceased’s other income for 2021. However you cannot reduce the taxable income to a negative amount. If you decide not to use the total of this balance in 2021, you can use the amount that is left to reduce other income for 2020.

Apply net capital losses against other types of income in the final return

Unlike in other tax years, remaining capital losses in the final return and the return for the year before the final year can be applied to other forms of income.

Apply net capital losses from the final year

If there are capital losses in line 12700 from calculating capital gains in the final return or from a carry back from the first year of a GRE, you can apply them to reduce other income in the final return (or return from the year before the death) after using one of the following calculation methods:

Method A: Reduce previous year capital gains before applying against other income

Using Method A, you may first reduce capital gains from the last 3 tax years before the year of death, before you apply the remaining net capital losses against other income.

How to carry a loss back to a prior year return

You can carry back a 2021 net capital loss to reduce any taxable capital gains in any of the three tax years before the year of death. If you are applying it against taxable capital gains realized in 2018, 2019, or 2020, you do not need to make any adjustment because the inclusion rate is the same in all three years. The loss you carry back cannot be more than the taxable capital gains in those years.

To ask for a loss carryback, complete “Section III – Net capital loss for carryback” on Form T1A, Request for Loss Carryback, and send it to your tax centre. Do not file an amended return for the year to which you want to apply the loss.

Subtract previous year capital gains deductions

After you carry back the loss, there may be an amount left.

You may be able to use some of the remaining amount to reduce other income on the final return, the return for the year before the year of death, or both returns. However, before you do this, you have to calculate the amount you can use.

From the net capital loss you have left, subtract any capital gains deductions the deceased has claimed to date.

Apply the remainder to reduce other income in the final return

Use any loss left to reduce other income for the year of death, the year before the year of death, or for both years.

If you claim any remaining net capital loss in the year of death, you should claim it as a negative amount in brackets at line 12700 of the final return.

You cannot reduce some income types with a capital loss

Do not use a capital loss claimed against other income at line 12700 in the calculation of net income for the purposes of calculating other amounts such as social benefit repayments, provincial or territorial tax credits, and those non-refundable tax credits requiring the use of net income.

Method B: Apply against other income in the final year and/or the previous year

You can choose not to carry back the net capital loss to reduce taxable capital gains from earlier years. You may prefer to reduce other income on the final return, the return for the year before the year of death, or both returns.

Subtract previous year capital gains deductions

However, before you do this, you have to calculate the amount you can use.

From the net capital loss, subtract any capital gains deductions the deceased has claimed to date.

Apply the remainder to reduce other income in the final return

Use any loss remaining to reduce other income for the year of death, the year before the year of death, or for both years.

If you claim any remaining net capital loss in the year of death, you should claim it as a negative amount in brackets at line 12700 of the final return.

Example comparison of Methods A and B

A man died on June 20, 2021. You have the following details about his tax matters:

  • Net capital loss in 2021: $11,000
  • Taxable capital gains in 2019: $4,000
  • Taxable capital gains in 2018: $2,000
  • Total capital gains deductions claimed to date: $4,000

He did not claim any capital gains deductions for 2018 or 2019.

You can use Method A or Method B.

Method A

If you choose Method A, you can use the net capital losses in 2021 to reduce his 2019 taxable capital gains to zero ($11,000 – $4,000). Then, you can use the remaining balance of $7,000 to reduce his 2018 taxable capital gain to zero ($7,000 – $2,000).

After you subtract his capital gains deductions ($5,000 – $4,000), you still have $1,000 left to reduce the man’s other income for 2021 or 2020 or for both years.

Method B

If you choose to use this method, you will first deduct his capital gains deductions of $4,000 from his net capital loss in 2021 of $11,000. You can now use the remaining $7,000 to reduce the man’s other income for 2021 or 2020, or for both years.

Request an adjustment to a previous year return

If you claim any remaining net capital loss in the year before the year of death, you will need to complete Form T1-ADJ, T1 Adjustment Request, or send the CRA a signed letter providing the details of your request.

Send your Form T1-ADJ or letter separately from the deceased’s final return. Applying a 2021 net capital loss to a previous year may reduce any capital gains deductions the deceased claimed in that year or a following year.

Apply unclaimed losses from previous tax years

Before you can apply capital losses from previous tax years to other income for the year of death or the year before the year of death, you must subtract all capital gains deductions claimed by the deceased in previous tax years.

Do not adjust previous year losses when applying them against other income.

Claim the losses on Line 25300 - Net capital losses of other years

Apply net capital losses in a T3 trust return

The estate might have a capital loss in line 1 after doing the capital gains calculation for a T3.

Net capital losses of an estate can be deducted on line 34 of the T3 trust return.

You can apply the net capital loss against the trust's taxable capital gains in any of:

  • the three preceding year trust returns
  • the trust's future tax year
  • the T1 final return of the person who died with the 164(6) election to carry back for net losses from the estate's first year

For more details on how to apply net capital losses in a T3, refer to section 4 of the T4013 T3 Trust Guide 2021.

Request for Loss Carryback by a Trust

To request to carry back an unused loss of a trust to a previous trust tax year:

  • the trust would file Form T3A, Request for Loss Carryback by a Trust in connection with the loss year to request the loss be carried back to the prior year
  • the trust would file Form T3-ADJ, T3 Adjustment Request for the prior year to reflect a late subsection 104(13.1) or (13.2) designation so as to amend the trust’s T3 return
  • the trust would issue amended T3 slips to the beneficiary for that prior year, reducing the income allocated
  • the beneficiaries would file a Form T1-ADJ, T1 Adjustment Request to reflect the revised T3 slip and to amend the T1 return

Listed personal property losses

Losses on listed personal property (LPP) can be applied only against LPP gains. For more details, refer to listed personal property losses.
