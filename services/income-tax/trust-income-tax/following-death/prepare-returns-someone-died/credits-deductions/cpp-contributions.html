CPP and QPP contributions

In addition to the usual calculations for CPP and QPP contributions, there are some additional steps when calculating for the final and optional returns of someone who died.

On this page

  • Determine where to calculate and report
  • Prorate the maximum pensionable earnings and the basic exemption
  • Claim CPP or QPP deduction on optional returns

Determine where to calculate and report

The calculation you will make depends on whether the deceased had employment or self-employment income and whether they contributed to both the CPP and QPP or just CPP.

  • Use schedule 8 of the T1, if you only need to calculate CPP contributions or if you only need to calculate QPP contributions.
  • Use RC381 Inter-provincial Calculation for CPP and QPP Contributions and Overpayments, if you need to calculate both CPP and QPP contributions.
Employment

Allowable employment contributions are calculated in Part 3 of schedule 8 or RC381

Report on lines:

  • 22215 (deduction for enhanced contributions on employment income)
  • 30800 (contributions through employment)
  • 44800 (CPP/QPP overpayment)
Self-employment only

Allowable and required contributions for self-employed and other earnings are calculated in Part 4 of schedule 8

Report on lines:

  • 22200 (deduction for contributions on self-employment other earnings)
  • 31000 (contributions on self-employment other earnings)
  • 42100 (required CPP contributions. For residents of Quebec, this amount is reported on the Quebec return.)
  • 44800 (CPP/QPP overpayment)
Self-employment in addition to employment

Allowable and required contributions on self-employment income when you also have employment income are calculated in Part 5 of schedule 8 or part 5 of RC381 if the taxpayer is a resident of Quebec, or part 4 of RC381 if the taxpayer is not a resident of Quebec.

Report on lines:

  • 22215 (deduction for enhanced contributions on employment income)
  • 30800 (contributions through employment)
  • 22200 (deduction for contributions on self-employment other earnings)
  • 31000 (contributions on self-employment other earnings)
  • 42100 (required CPP contributions. For residents of Quebec, this amount is reported on the Quebec return.)
  • 44800 (CPP/QPP overpayment)

Overpayments

Overpayments to CPP or QPP are reported on 44800.

If there are only employment earnings, overpayments are any negative amounts you calculate by subtracting the total required contributions from the total contributions withheld.

If there are self-employed and other earnings and the calculated required contributions is a negative amount, the overpayment is 50% of the negative amount.

Prorate the maximum pensionable earnings and the basic exemption

In the year of death, the maximum pensionable earnings and the basic exemption for the calendar year are prorated. If the only reason to prorate is due to the death of the taxpayer, these amounts are found on the monthly proration table in schedule 8 or RC381.

The proration calculation for a deceased person who did not stop receiving CPP or QPP disability benefits or, for CPP, did not revoke a previous election to stop making CPP benefits during the year:

  • Maximum pensionable earnings or basic exemption
  • divided by 12 (months in a calendar year)
  • multiplied by The number of months from January up to and including the month of death
  • equals Prorated maximum pensionable earnings or basic exemption
Example of how to prorate the CPP or QPP earnings and exemption

If the date of death is in May, for 2021, the maximum pensionable earnings would be $25,667 ($61,600 divided by 12 and multiplied by 5 months) and the basic exemption would be $1,458 ($3,500 by 12 and multiplied by 5 months).

If the deceased had been receiving CPP or QPP disability benefits but stopped receiving them in February the number of months used in the prorating calculation would be 4 as CPP/QPP contributions were not required in January.

If the deceased person had revoked a previous election to stop making CPP contributions in February, the number of months used in the prorating calculation for CPP would be 3 as CPP contributions were not required for January and February.

Claim CPP or QPP deductions on an optional return

You may be able to claim CPP and QPP deductions of the deceased on an optional return if income is also reported there:

  • If employment income is reported on an optional return for rights or things 70(2), you may claim deductions for contributions on lines 2215 and 30800.
  • If self-employment income is reported on an optional return for a partner or proprietor 150(4), you may claim deductions for contributions on lines 22200 and 31000.

On the first T1 return filed, (generally the final return 70(1)), if the employment or self-employment earnings and contributions withheld from the other return are available, enter the total amount of the earnings. Otherwise enter the amount from the first return.

On the second return filed (generally the optional return for rights or things 70(2) or optional return for a partner or proprietor 150(4)) enter the pensionable employment or self-employment earnings and contributions withheld from both returns.

The amounts allowed in each of lines 22215, 22200, 30800, 31000, 42100 and 44800 is the calculated amount minus what was reported on other returns.

The amount allowed in the previous returns is determined by the prorated maximum pensionable earnings and the basic exemption.
