RRIFs

How an RRIF is treated at the time of the annuitant’s death depends on who it is transferred to and if any income was earned after the date of death.

Report RRIF income and value

Generally, the CRA considers a deceased annuitant to have received, immediately before death, an amount equal to the fair market value (FMV) of all the property held in the RRIF at the time of death. The FMV is shown on the T4RIF slip.

If the deceased was 65 or older, or if the deceased was under 65 and received the RRIF payments due to the death of their spouse or common-law partner, see Line 11500 - Other pensions or superannuation.

Is the spouse or common-law partner, or an RDSP account designated as the beneficiary of the registered account in the RRIF contract or will?

Yes - rollover to a spouse or common-law partner
Found on slip
T4RIF - box 16 (issued to the spouse or common-law partner)
Report on line
11500 of the spouse or common-law's personal income tax return

2 conditions must be met:

  • spouse or common-law partner is named as sole beneficiary of the RRIF contract, and
  • entire RRIF property is directly transferred, by December 31 of the year following the year of death, into a registered plan (RRSP, PRPP SPP, or RRIF) in which the spouse or common-law partner is the annuitant, or to an issuer to buy an eligible annuity

  • Claim the deduction
    • If the property was directly transferred to the spouse or common-law partner’s RRSP, PRPP or SPP, they can claim a deduction on line 20800 of their Income tax and benefit return.
    • If the amount was transferred to their RRIF, RDSP or annuity, claim a deduction equal to the amount transferred, on line 23200 of their Income tax and benefit return.

    • Example scenario

      Sarah was born on July 14, 1951. She died on December 10, 2019 at the age of 68. When she died the FMV of her trusteed RRIF was $150,000. The FMV of the RRIF on December 31, 2019, was $160,000. The distribution of the RRIF property was delayed until 2021.

      The RRIF contract designated Sarah’s husband, Dan, as the sole beneficiary of the RRIF. On June 30, 2021, he received $165,000 from the RRIF carrier.

      Dan, who is also the legal representative of Sarah’s estate, received the two following slips from the RRIF carrier:

      • a T4RIF slip for 2021 issued in his name, showing $10,000 in box 16 and $5,000 in boxes 22 and 36
      • a T4RIF slip for 2019 issued in Sarah's name, showing $150,000 in box 18.

        • (Although Dan is the sole beneficiary of the RRIF, the slip was issued to Sarah because she did not meet the 2 conditions.)

      Had Sarah not died, the minimum payment under the RRIF for 2021 would have been $7,272.73. Dan decides that it would be beneficial to ask for a reduction to the amount Sarah is considered to have received from her RRIF. This would allow him to shift some of her income onto his income tax and benefit return.

      After filling out Chart 2, Dan decides to claim a $130,000 reduction. This reduces the amount reported on line 13000 of Sarah’s 2019 income tax and benefit return to $20,000 ($150,000 – $130,000). Because the FMV of the RRIF at the time of death was included in Sarah’s income for 2019, Dan has to write a letter to request an adjustment to that year’s return. Dan has to report $145,000 ($130,000 + $10,000 + $5,000) on line 11500 of his 2021 income tax and benefit return.

      To minimize his 2021 taxes, Dan decides to transfer the eligible part of his designated benefit to his RRIF. The amount that qualifies as a designated benefit is $140,000 ($145,000 – $5,000). Dan fills out Chart 3, and determines that he can transfer $132,734 to his RRIF. He claims a $132,734 deduction on line 23200 of his 2020 income tax and benefit return.

Yes - rollover to a Registered Disability Savings Plan (RDSP)
Found on slip
T4RIF - box 22 (issued to the deceased annuitant)
Report on line
12500 of the deceased annuitant's final income tax return

The existing RRIF rollover rules allow a specified RDSP payment from the deceased person's RRIF to the RDSP of an eligible individual.

An eligible individual is a child or grandchild of a deceased annuitant under an RRSP or a RRIF, or of a deceased member of a registered pension plan (RPP), PRPP, or SPP who was financially dependent on the deceased for support at the time of the deceased's death, because of an impairment in physical or mental function.

A specified RDSP payment is a payment that meets all of the following conditions:

    • the payment is made to an RDSP under which the eligible individual is the beneficiary
    • the payment satisfies the contribution conditions described in Guide RC4460, Registered Disability Savings Plan
    • effective March 19, 2019, if the eligible individual no longer qualifies for the disability tax credit, the payment is made before the end of the fifth taxation year throughout which the beneficiary is ineligible for the disability tax credit 

    Form RC4625, Rollover to a Registered Disability Savings Plan (RDSP) Under Paragraph 60(m), must be attached to both the deceased annuitant's and the eligible individual's income tax and benefit returns.

    In these situations, you will not have to fill out a Schedule 7, RRSP, PRPP, and SPP Unused Contributions, Transfers, and HBP or LLP Activities. However, you must attach to the income tax and benefit return the receipt indicating the amount of the rollover.

    The amount that can be rolled over to an RDSP cannot exceed the RDSP lifetime limit of $200,000.

No - elect to have RRIF payments rolled over to the spouse or common-law partner

If the surviving spouse or common-law partner is the beneficiary of the estate, that person and the legal representative can jointly elect, in writing, to treat the amounts the RRIF paid to the estate as being paid to the spouse or common-law partner.

The RRIF carrier must also agree to contine the payments under the deceased annuitant's RRIF to the surviving spouse or common-law partner.

  • Attach a copy of the written election to the return of the surviving spouse or common-law partner. The election has to specify that this person is electing to become the annuitant of the RRSP.
    • No - transfer to another beneficiary or the estate
    Found on slip
    T4RIF - box 18 (issued to the deceased annuitant)
    Report on line
    13000 of the deceased annuitant's final income tax return

    At the time of the annuitant’s death, the FMV of all property held in the RRIF is included in their income for the year of death.

    The amount reported on the deceased annuitant's final income tax return might be reduced if an amount is paid from the unmatured RRSP to either you or the estate of which you are the beneficiary. At the time of death, you must be either:

  • the spouse or common-law partner of the annuitant
  • a financially dependant child or grandchild of the annuitant

    • Did the value of the registered account increase or decrease between the date of death and when it was paid to a beneficiary?

    Yes - increased in value
    Found on slip
    T4RIF - box 24 (issued to the beneficiary or the estate)
    Report on line
    13000 of the beneficiary or the estate's income tax return

    Sometimes there is an increase in the value of an RRIF between the date of death and the date of final distribution to the beneficiary or estate.

    • This amount has to be included in the income of the beneficiary or the estate for the year it is received. A T4RIF slip will be issued for this amount.
    Yes - decreased in value

    Sometimes, the FMV of the property of an RRIF decreases between the date of death and the date of final distribution to the beneficiary or the estate.

    • If the total of all distributions from the RRIF is less than the FMV of the property that was included in the deceased annuitant's income for the year of death, the deceased's legal representative can request that the difference be deducted on the deceased's final return.
    • Generally, for the deduction to be allowed, the final distribution must occur by the end of the year that follows the year of death.
    No - there was no change in value

    If the value of the RRIF did not change between the date of death and distribution to a beneficiary, there are no further amounts to report.
