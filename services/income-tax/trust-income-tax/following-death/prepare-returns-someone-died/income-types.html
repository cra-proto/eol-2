Get access to the deceased's tax slips and previous filings

Most income can be found on the tax slips of the person who died. As the legal or authorised representative, you can get access to most tax slips through Represent a Client. Alternatively, you can contact the CRA by phone.

You can also find previous T1 filings of the deceased. Previous T3 filings are, however, not yet available in Represent a Client.

Income on the final and optional returns

The income lines are the same on the final income tax return as well as the optional returns.

If you choose not to file the optional returns, simply include all income earned up to the date of death in the final return 70(1), even if it is received after the date of death. If you would like to file the optional returns, refer to how to report income on the optional returns.

Income on a T3 return

All income types that can be reported on a T1 can also be reported on a T3. Include any income the estate earned after death in a T3.

You might need to file T3 returns for more than one year, if the estate continues to earn income in tax years after the year of death.
