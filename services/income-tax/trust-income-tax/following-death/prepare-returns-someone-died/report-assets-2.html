What returns are required for someone who died

As the representative of someone who died, you might be required to file a T1 income tax return for the final year as well as a T3 for each year after the death that the estate of the deceased continues to receive income.

T1 Income Tax and Benefits Return
When you are required to file

Any income a person who died earned between January 1 and the date of death must be reported on a T1 Income Tax and Benefits Return.

There are 4 types of T1 returns you can file for the final year:

  • Final return 70(1): The final return is required, and you can submit it online.
  • Optional returns: If you are eligible to file an optional return, you may choose to report certain types of eligible income that would otherwise go on the final return on an optional return. You can only submit it by mail.

Complete the T1 Income Tax Package for the final and each optional return you file.

T3 Trust Income Tax and Information Return
When you are required to file

Any income earned after the date of death, is considered income of the estate of the person who died and must be reported on a T3 Trust Income and Information Return.

What is an estate

After someone has died, their belongings, property, assets and liabilities are called an estate. Estates of someone who died generally are designated as a Graduated Rate Estate (GRE).

An estate will continue to exist and require T3 returns for any income it earns until it is fully distributed to beneficiaries.

Graduated Rate Estate (GRE)

A Graduated Rate Estate is an estate that arose on and as a consequence of an individual’s death, if that time is no more than 36 months after the death of the individual, and the estate is at that time a testamentary trust that meets all of the following conditions:

  • The estate designates itself as the person who died's GRE in its T3 Trust Income Tax and Information Return (T3 Return) for its first taxation year.
  • No other estate designates itself as the GRE of the person who died.
  • The estate must include the person who died’s social insurance number in its T3 return of income for each taxation year of the estate that ends after 2015 during the 36-month period after the death of the individual.

Benefits of a Graduated Rate Estate

A Graduated Rate Estate has the following benefits:

  1. Income earned by a GRE is taxed at the same graduated tax rates as individual taxpayers
  2. Tax credits resulting from charitable donations made from a GRE after the death or in the will can be carried back to previous GRE tax years or to the final return

Other types of testamentary trusts

After 3 years, or if there are other reasons the estate is not designated a GRE, the estate will become a different type of testamentary trust.

If the estate continues beyond the year of death, you will need to continue filing T3 returns for estate income in subsequent years.

You might not need to file a T3 return if the only income after death can be reported by a beneficiary. For example, the CPP Death Benefit can be reported on the beneficiary's return instead of a T3.

Complete the T3 Income Tax and Information Return if you need to report income of an estate.

Income eligible to report on an optional return

Although you are not required to file any of the optional returns, you may reduce or eliminate tax that you would otherwise have to pay for the person who died if you do so.

If you choose to file any of the optional returns, report all income that is eligible to be included in that optional return in the optional return instead of the final return.

The optional returns use the same T1 return form as the final return, and have the same income line numbers as the final return. Learn how to file the optional returns.

The difference is that you can only report income from specific sources and time periods in the optional returns. They are as follows:

Return for rights or things 70(2)

You can only report income that was received after death that, had the person not died, would have been included in their usual income tax return. This does not include capital gains.

Types of eligible income
  • salary, commissions, and vacation pay owed before the death and paid after death
    • owed to the person who died by the employer on the date of death for a pay period that ended before the date of death
    • in some cases salary, commissions and vacation pay might not be taxable. Refer to chart 3 - non-taxable amounts in the T4011 Preparing Returns for Deceased Persons 2021.
  • retroactive salary adjustments owed and paid after death
    • this does not include some retroactive contract settlements payable after the date of death
  • Old Age Security (OAS), Canada Pension Plan/Quebec Pension Plan (CPP/QPP) paid after the date of death for the month of death
  • CPP and Employment Insurance (EI) arrears
  • accounts receivable, supplies, and inventory
    • accounts receivable, supplies on hand, and inventory are rights or things if the business of the person who died used the cash method. The inventory of an artist who has elected to value their inventory at nil is also a rights or things.
  • uncashed matured bond coupons
  • bond interest earned but not received before death
  • dividends declared before the date of death, but not received
  • crops, livestock
    • this includes harvested farm crops and livestock that is not part of the basic herd. For more information, see Archived Interpretation Bulletins IT234, Income of Deceased Persons - Farm Crops, and IT427R,  Livestock of Farmers.
  • work in progress
    • work in progress is a right or thing if the person who died was a sole proprietor and a professional (accountant, dentist, lawyer [in Quebec an advocate or notary], medical doctor, veterinarian, or chiropractor) who had elected to exclude work in progress when calculating their total income. For more information about rights or things, see Archived Interpretation Bulletin IT212R3, Income of Deceased Persons - Rights or things, and its Special Release.
Example of rights or things

Casey passed away on March 2nd 2020. On March 12th, Casey's estate received income from her job earned between January 1 to March 1st.

The income that Casey received while she was alive must be reported on Casey's final personal income tax return, 70(1). The income Casey was paid on March 12, after her death, but earned for work in her last week, can be reported on either the final personal income tax return, 70(1), or an optional rights and things return, 70(2).

Return for a partner or proprietor 150(4)

You may report in the optional return any income received between the fiscal year end and the date of death if the person who died met both of the following criteria:

  • the person who died was a sole proprietor or partner
  • their business had a fiscal year end that is not December 31.
Example of eligible income for a proprietor

A person who had a business died on May 28, 2021. The business has a March 31 fiscal year-end.

You have two choices when you report the person's 2021 income:

  • include the business income from April 1, 2020, to May 28, 2021 on the final return.
  • file a return for a partner or proprietor in addition to the final return.
    • On the final return, include business income from April 1, 2020, to March 31, 2021.
    • On the return for a partner or proprietor, report the business income from April 1, 2021, to May 28, 2021.
Return for a Graduated Rate Estate (GRE) 104(23)(d)

If income was received from a Graduated Rate Estate of another person who died (generally found on a T3 slip) between the fiscal year end of the GRE and the date of death.

Example of income from a GRE

A husband gets income from a testamentary trust with a fiscal year from April 1 to March 31. The trust was formed as a result of his wife's death on March 31, 2020 and designates itself as a GRE of the wife in its return of income for March 31, 2021. The husband died on June 11, 2021.

You have two choices when you report the husband's income from the trust:

  • Include the husband's income from the GRE from April 1, 2020, to June 11, 2021, on his final return.
  • File a return for income received from the GRE in addition to the final return.
    • On the husband's final return, include the income from the GRE from April 1, 2020, to March 31, 2021.
    • On the optional return for income from the GRE, report the income from April 1, 2021, to June 11, 2021.

File a previous year return

If a return from any year before the year of the death was not filed, the legal representative must also ensure it is filed.
