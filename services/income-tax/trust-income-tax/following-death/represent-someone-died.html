Determine who is representing the person who died

When someone dies, a representative is responsible for the adminstration of the estate of the person who died. The CRA requires that the representative file tax returns and resolves any balances owing of the person's account.

Types of representatives

Legal representative

The legal representative is typically the executor named in the will who will be handling the estate. The will provides instruction if there are more than one executor named and all other matters of the estate.

The estate is the value of the financial assets, such as property and investments, and financial liabilities, such as balances owing to the CRA, of the person who died.

In Québec, you are the legal representative if you are the liquidator of the estate and may have additional administrative duties.

If you are the legal representative, you must confirm you are the legal representative with the CRA

Authorized representative

The legal representative may also choose to appoint one or more authorized representatives to manage the tax information on their behalf.

An authorized representive is someone who can view, get information about and update the tax information for individuals or businesses. This is typically an accountant or a lawyer who would need access to the tax information.

Find out how to authorize a representative.

Remove an authorized representative

If the person who died had authorized representatives for their tax accounts while they were alive, those authorized representatives will continue to be authorized on the account until a legal representative requests their removal.

If they are no longer a representative of the deceased, the legal representative may cancel authorization for a representative.

Check if there is a will

The will is typically where you will find information about who will represent the person who died and instructions on what to do with the estate.

Yes, there is a will with a named executor

If you are the executor of a will, you are the legal representative of the person who died and responsible for the adminstration of the estate. In some cases, a court document may also be issued appointing an executor.

You will need to provide a copy of the will or court document to the CRA to show that you are the legal representative.

No, there is not a will or a named executor

If a person dies without a will (intestate), or an executor has not been named in a will, certain individuals can apply to adminster the estate. Check with estate law of provincial or terriortial government of the residence of the person who died for more information.

If there is no court-appointed representive or you are waiting for an appointment, you can request to be appointed as a representative for CRA matters using form RC552 Appointing a Representative for a Deceased Person.

Once your request has been approved, you will be authorized as a representative who can view, get information about and update the tax information for the person who died. This approval is for CRA purposes only and not for other adminstrative responsibilities outside the CRA.

Responsibilities of the legal representative

The legal representative is responsible for doing the taxes of the person who died and making sure any balance owing is paid before distributing the estate.

As the legal representative, you need to:

  • make sure the CRA has been notified of the death
  • confirm you are the legal representative with the CRA
  • provide the CRA with any requested documentation
    • make sure tax returns are filed for the person who died:
      • any returns for the year the person died
      • any T3 Trust Income Tax and Information returns, if required
      • any returns for previous years that the person who died did not file
  • confirm the estate resolved all outstanding balances owing to the CRA
  • apply for a clearance certificate, as needed, before distributing any assets of the estate
    • the legal representative can be held personally liable for outstanding tax balances owing, if assets have been distributed without getting a clearance certificate
    • if the estate cannot pay its debts, a clearance certificate is not needed

The social insurance number (SIN) of the person who died must be provided with any request or documents you are sending to the CRA.

If the person who died was a resident of Québec, you may have additional responsibilities with Revenu Québec. Visit Revenu Québec's steps to follow after a death for information.

Confirm the legal representative with the CRA

As the legal representative, you must submit documents to the CRA to confirm that you are the legal representative and provide contact information for the estate:

Online

Follow these steps to confirm you are the legal representative:

  1. Register for Represent a Client
    • Registering will give you a Representative ID, and access to an account where you can upload documents
  2. Submit the documents to confirm you are the legal representative through Represent a Client

What documents you need to submit

  • a copy of the death certificate, or a funeral director's statement of death
  • a complete copy of the will
    • if there is no will, include another document showing that you are the legal representative, usually grant of probate or letters of administration
  • the current mailing address of the estate
  • a completed Request for the Canada Revenue Agency to Update Records form

Include the SIN number of the deceased in the documents you send in.

Request access the tax records of the deceased

Write and submit a letter requesting access to the tax records of the person who died. Include your RepID and the SIN of the person who died.

This can be submitted when you confirm the legal representative or at a later date.

By mail or fax
Send a letter to confirm you are the legal representative and get access to tax records

To confirm you are the legal representative by mail or fax, you must send the same documents as you would in an online request.

Send the documents to your tax centre.

Request access the tax records of the deceased

Write and mail a letter requesting access to the tax records of the person who died and send it to the mailing address of your tax centre. Include the SIN of the person who died in your request. This can be submitted when you confirm the legal representative or at a later date.

You can also call 1-800-959-8281 after you have sent in documents confirming the legal representative and those documents have been approved. Make sure you have the SIN of the person who died when you call.

Online access to records is only available through Represent a Client.

Authorize additional representatives

The legal representative can also authorize additional representatives, such as accountants or lawyers and grant them access to the tax records of the deceased.

How do you want to authorize the representative?

Authorize online access to taxpayer records in Represent a Client

Option 1: Add an authorized representative from the legal representative's Represent a Client

If the both representatives have a RepID and are registered for Represent a Client, the legal representative may add another representative in their Represent a Client.

  1. Go to Manage authorized representatives under the Represent a Client Personal profile and select Authorize new representative.
  2. Follow the steps to add an authorized representative.
    • You will need the authorized representative’s RepID, Business number or GroupID.
  3. Once you complete the steps to add them, you will receive a confirmation and they will have access to the account immediately.

Option 2: The additional representative can submit an authorization request electronically

If both the additional representative and the legal representative have registered for Represent a Client, a request can be made for access to the deceased's tax files.

  1. The additional representative should submit an authorization request through their Represent a Client.
    • An additional representative cannot submit an authorization request in Represent a Client for a deceased client until we receive and process a copy of the deceased person's last will and testament.
  2. Within 10 days, you will need to review, validate, and either confirm or deny the electronic authorization request or it will be cancelled. If you still want to authorize a representative, you can have your representative submit a new request.
    • The authorization request will appear with a pending status in the Authorized Representative(s) section under Manage authorized representatives which you can access from the Personal profile in the legal representative's Represent a Client.
  3. After you confirm the additional representative's request, it will can take up to 1 business day for them to have access from their Represent a Client.

Option 3: Give an additional representative access if the legal representative doesn't have Represent a Client

If the legal representative is not registered for Represent a Client, you can follow these steps to authorize an additional representative:

  1. Provide the additional representative with information from the deceased’s notice of assessment from a previous return that was processed at least six months ago.
  2. The additional representative should submit an authorization request through their Represent a Client.
    • An additional representative cannot submit an authorization request in Represent a Client for a deceased client until we receive and process a copy of the deceased person's last will and testament.
  3. They will have to print the request summary, referred to as a certification page and get the legal representative to sign this document.
  4. The additional representative should submit the document in Represent a Client.
    • Do not mail or fax a copy of the certification page to us. These copies will not be processed or returned to you.
  5. It will take up to 3 business days for us to review the file after which we may call the legal representative to confirm the request.
Authorize a representative for offline access

To authorize a representative to communicate on your behalf with the CRA by phone, mail or in-person:

  1. Download and complete AUT-01 Authorize a Representative for Offline Access
  2. Submit the form, either through:
    • Represent a Client's submit a document service
    • By mail to your tax centre
