Retirement or death of business owner or partner

Depending on your business structure, the retirement or death of an owner could have an impact on your CRA business accounts.

Sole proprietorship or partnership

Determine if the business is being closed

When the owner of a sole proprietorship passes away, the representative of the estate will have to close the business.

In the case of a partnership, the status of the business account depends entirely on the type of partnership agreement that has been written. It is possible to bring in partners without having to register for a new BN. If a partner no longer wants to be in a partnership, you might need a change of owner, partners or directors.

If it is determined that the partnership or sole-properitorship will cease to exist, follow the steps to close the business.

Steps to close a sole-proprietorship or partnership

Before the business can be closed, a proprietor, partner or legal representative needs to:

  1. File the final income tax return

    • Report self-employment income in a personal income tax return

      Self-employment includes income from a business, a profession, commission, farming, or fishing.

    • Farm and fishing businesses

      If you are closing a farm or fishing business, you might qualify for capital gains exemptions.

  2. Close the business number (BN) and business accounts (payroll, GST/HST)

    After the final return and all the amounts owing have been processed, you will need to close the business number (BN) as well as program accounts such as for payroll or GST/HST.

    Ensure that you calculate any GST/HST owed, including on the deemed disposition of the business's capital property (inventory and equipment) when you close the account.

  3. Apply for a clearance certificate before you transfer the business's assets

    If you closed a GST/HST account, you will need to apply for a GST/HST clearance certificate in addition to an income tax clearance certificate.

Corporation

1. Determine where it's incorporated - provincial or federal 2. Go to incorporating authority and determine if you're going to amalgamate, change directors (ownder) of the business or disolve the business 3. Based on your decision, change the director with BE - or close program accounts If dissolution - then file T2 and clearance certificates

Determine if the corporation is being closed

When the sole remaining director of a corporation retires or passes away, the corporation can continue to operate. The structure of the corporation will determine the outcome of the corporation's existence.

Follow the following steps if the corporation is being closed.

If the corporation continues

Steps to close a corporation

  1. Change of owner, partners or directors

    In the case of a death of the corporation's owner, the legal representative will have to update the board of directors so that they can file income taxes and close accounts.

  2. File a T2 income tax return

    Report income from the final year of the corporation's operations in a T2 corporate income tax return.

  3. Apply for a clearance certificate before you transfer the business's assets

    If you closed a GST/HST account, you will need to apply for a GST/HST clearance certificate in addition to an income tax clearance certificate. You will also need to apply for a clearance certificate for the T2 return.
