Types of returns for someone who died

In addition to the income tax return, you might need to file a T3 or returns for the province of Quebec.

Final personal income tax return 70(1)

Who should file: Generally, everyone must file a final personal tax return. This is similar to your yearly income tax return but has some differences depending on the assets of the person who died.

Deadline:

The later of:

  • April 30 of the year following the death (the date of death falls between January and October)
  • 6 months following the death on the same calendar day as the death (the date of death falls during November or December)
Extended deadline if self-employed (running a business)

If the person who died or their spouse or common-law partner was running a business on the date of death, your deadline for the final return is extended to the later of:

  • June 15 of the year following the death (the date of death falls between January 1 and December 16)
  • 6 months following the death on the same calendar day as the death (the date of death falls between December 17 and December 31)

You will not be considered eligible for this extended due date if the business's expenditures are mainly for cost or capital cost of tax shelter investments.

Carrying a loss back from the T3 Trust Income Tax and Information Return

If you have a loss in your T3 Trust Income Tax and Information Return, you might be eligible to elect under 164(6) of the Income Tax Act to carry that loss back to your personal income tax return.

Optional returns for someone who died

Who should file: Although you are not required to file any of the optional returns, you may reduce or eliminate tax that you would otherwise have to pay for the person who died if you do so.

Find out if you have eligible income to report on the optional returns.

Return for rights or things 70(2)
Deadline: 90 days after the mailing date of the Notice of Assessment or Reassessment for the final return.
Return for a partner or proprietor 150(4)
Deadline: You must file this by the same deadlines as the final personal income tax return, generally April 30.
Return for a Graduated Rate Estate (GRE) 104(23)(d)
Deadline: You must file this by the same deadlines as the final personal income tax return, generally April 30.

T3 Trust Income Tax and Information Return

Who should file: You might have to file a T3 Trust Income Tax and Information Return if the estate of the person who died receives income after the date of death. Should the estate remain open for multiple years, you might need to file a T3 for more than one tax year.

Deadline:

  • 90 days after the end of the trust's tax year - the end of the trust's tax year is generally the date the estate was distributed, or 1 day following the annual of the date of death, whichever comes first.

Returns for residents of Quebec

Who should file: You might have to file additional tax returns to the province of Quebec if the person who died was resident of Quebec during the year of the death.

Refer to Revenue Quebec for provincial filing requirements

Penalties if you file a return late

If you file a return late and there is a balance owing, the CRA will charge a late-filing penalty.

The penalty is 5% of any balance owing, plus 1% of the balance owing for each full month that the return is late, to a maximum of 12 months. The late-filing penalty may be higher if the CRA charged a late-filing penalty on a return of the deceased for any of the three previous years. The CRA will also charge you interest on both the balance owing and any penalty.
