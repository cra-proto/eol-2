When you need a clearance certificate

A clearance certificate lets the legal representative distribute assets. Your financial institution or lawyers may also ask you for a clearance certificate, depending on your situation.

The legal representative is the person who applies for the certificate.

For more information, see IC82-6R12 Clearance Certificate

For an estate of someone who died

You may need a clearance certificate if:

  • you need to distribute assets of a deceased individual
  • the person had a GST/HST number

You may not need a clearance certificate to distribute assets if:

  • you are able to keep enough assets aside to pay any amounts owing to CRA
  • the estate or trust continues to exist to pay income to the beneficiaries
  • the estate of someone who died is unable to pay debts owed to CRA
For a corporation

You may need a clearance certificate if:

  • the corporation is winding up or for any other type of corporate dissolution, you should get a clearance certificate before you distribute the corporate property.

You may not need a clearance certificate if:

  • the person had a GST/HST number

You may not need a clearance certificate to distribute assets if:

  • the corporation’s assets and liabilities are being rolled over into another corporation and there is no other tax liability
  • enough funds remain in the corporate account to pay amounts owing or potential amounts owing to the CRA

What can happen if you don't get a clearance certificate

If the legal representative does not get a clearance certificate before they distribute assets and there are any amounts owing to the CRA, they are personally liable for unpaid amounts.

If a certificate was obtained before distributing the estate, in rare cases, we might revoke the certificate if the legal representative provided inaccurate information as part of the application.

When to apply for a clearance certificate

A clearance certificate should be requested after:

  • all of the returns have been filed
  • you have received the notices of assessment from the returns
  • any outstanding balances to the CRA have been paid and there are no other outstanding adjustment requests

For someone who has died, CRA also needs to be notified of the death.

Do not submit your clearance certificate request at the same time as your returns filing. This will result in a delay of your returns assessed.

How to apply

Forms you need

For someone who has died (T1) or a trust (T3)

You will need to fill in a TX19 Asking for a Clearance Certificate form.

If the person who died also had a GST/HST number, you will also need to fill out the GST352 Application for Clearance Certificate.

If you have not previously sent us the following documents, submit them with your completed form:

  • a complete and signed copy of the taxpayer's will, including any codicils, renunciations, disclaimers, and all probate documents if applicable. If the taxpayer died intestate (without a will), attach a copy of the document appointing an administrator (for example, the Letters of Administration or Letters of Verification issued by a provincial court)
  • a copy of the trust agreement or documents for inter vivos trusts
  • any other documents that are necessary to prove that you are the legal representative
  • a detailed list of the assets that were owned by the person who died at the date of death
  • a detailed statement of distribution of the assets of the trust or the estate to date
  • a statement of proposed distribution of any holdback or residual amount or property
  • the names, addresses, and social insurance numbers of any beneficiaries of property other than cash
  • a completed Form T1013, Authorizing or Cancelling a Representative, signed by all legal representatives, authorizing a representative such as an accountant, notary, or lawyer if you want us to communicate with any other person or firm, or if you want the clearance certificate sent to any address other than your own.
For corporations

You will need to fill in a TX19 Asking for a Clearance Certificate form.

If the business also has a GST/HST account, you will also need to fill out GST352 Application for Clearance Certificate.

If you have not previously sent us the following documents, submit them with your completed form:

  • a copy of the director’s or shareholder’s resolution confirming the intention to dissolve the corporation and the date of dissolution
  • a copy of the notice of assessment for the final T2 filed
  • any other documents that are necessary to prove that you are the legal representative
  • a completed Form RC59, Business Consent, signed by all legal representatives, authorizing a representative such as an accountant or lawyer if you want us to communicate with any other person or firm, or if you want the clearance certificate sent to any address other than your own
  • a statement of distribution of the company assets to date as well as the scheme of the distribution of company assets at the date of wind-up
For GST/HST

You will need to complete Form GST352, Application for Clearance Certificate

Submit your request

You can submit the completed request by mail, fax, or online using the Submit Documents feature on My Account for Individuals,Represent a Client, and My Business Account.

Submit using My Account

Submit the completed form Asking for a Clearance Certificate (TX19, GST352) and supporting information via My Account.

As the legal representative (that is, Executor), you may login using your My Account access to submit Clearance Certificate requests that you are the legal representative for.

  1. Log into My Account.
  2. Select the “Submit Documents” option located in the upper right corner of the screen.
  3. At the bottom of the screen, click on “without a case or reference number”.
  4. Select submission reason:
    • Select “Send documents related to Certificate of Compliance, International Waivers, Tax Shelter Identification Number or Clearance Certificate”.
  5. Click the “Next” button.
  6. Select submission reason:
    • “Send request for Clearance Certificate (TX19, GST352), and supporting documents”.
  7. Click on the “Next” button.
  8. Select “Browse” and navigate to the required file(s).
    • Multiple files totalling up to 500MB can be attached by pressing and holding the CTRL key while selecting your files. A 10 file limit per session also applies.
  9. Enter a description of the file submission. This is a mandatory step.
  10. Select “Attach file”.
  11. When all files have been attached, click “Submit file(s)”.
  12. Record the Confirmation number and Case/reference number.

For help with income tax clearance certificates, call 1-800-959-8281.

For help with goods and services tax/harmonized sales tax (GST/HST) or T2 clearance certificates, call 1-800-959-5525.

Submit using Represent A Client

Submit the completed form Asking for a Clearance Certificate (TX19, GST352) and supporting information via Represent a Client.

Representatives do not need to have electronic authorization access to the specific clients account to submit clearance certificate requests via Represent a Client.

  1. Log into Represent a Client.
  2. Select the “Submit Documents” from the options on the left hand side of the screen.
  3. Select Client Type:
    • Individual Client (T1)
    • Business Client (T2) (GST/HST)
    • Trust Account (T3)
    • Representative related: Documents on behalf of yourself as a representative
  4. Select “No” to the question “Do you have a case or reference number?”.
  5. Click on the “Next” button.
  6. Select “Send request for Clearance Certificate (TX19, GST352), and supporting documents”.
  7. Select “Next”.
  8. Select “Browse” and navigate to the required file(s).
    • Multiple files totalling up to 500MB can be attached by pressing and holding the CTRL key while selecting your files. A 10 file limit per session also applies.
  9. Enter a description of the file submission. This is a mandatory step.
  10. Select “Attach file”.
  11. When all files have been attached, click “Submit file(s)”.
  12. Record the Confirmation number and Case/reference number.

For help with income tax clearance certificates, call 1-800-959-8281.

For help with goods and services tax/harmonized sales tax (GST/HST) or T2 clearance certificates, call 1-800-959-5525.

Submit using My Business Account

Submit the completed form Asking for a Clearance Certificate (TX19, GST352) and supporting information via My Business Account.

  1. Log into My Business Account.
  2. Select the “Submit Documents” option located in the upper right corner of the screen.
  3. At the bottom of the screen, click “No case or reference number?”.
  4. From the drop-down menu, select ”Send request for Clearance Certificate (TX19, GST352), and supporting documents”.
  5. Click on the “Next” button.
  6. Select the program account number associated with the document(s) you are submitting from the drop-down menu.
  7. Click the “Next” button.
  8. Select “Browse” and navigate to the required file(s).
    • Multiple files totalling up to 500MB can be attached by pressing and holding the CTRL key while selecting your files.
  9. Enter a description of the file submission. This is a mandatory step.
  10. Select “Upload file”.
  11. When all files have been attached, click “Submit file(s)”.
  12. Record the Confirmation number and Case/reference number.

For help with income tax clearance certificates, call 1-800-959-8281.

For help with goods and services tax/harmonized sales tax (GST/HST) or T2 clearance certificates, call 1-800-959-5525.

Processing times

We will send the representative of the person who died an acknowledgement letter within 45 days of receiving the request for a clearance certificate.

Our assessment can take up to 120 days, assuming all of the necessary documents were provided. However, in certain situations, the CRA may need to do an audit before it issues the clearance certificate.

If you have not received your certificate or a clearance certificate officer has not contacted you within 120 days, contact enquiries at:

For individual (T1), or trust (T3) accounts call 1-800-959-8281

For corporations (T2) or GST/HST accounts call 1-800-959-5525
